Partnerships

Giving Back to the Community

Holcombe L. Rucker High School engages in local and regional partnerships to further our mission and expand educational opportunities for our students. With each partnership, we strive to give our students the help and guidance they need while enrolled in school and/or after graduation.

For over 30 years, The School of Cooperative Technical Education is a half-day vocational program that offers students hands on training in a variety of in demand and cutting edge trade areas. Coop Tech serves approximately 1,500 students who are 17 to 21 years of age and who represent a wide variety of ethic and cultural backgrounds, disabilities, and educatonal histories. Coop Tech students join us as either shared instruction students who also attend over 130 traditional high schools and Pathways to Graduation programs or as post-graduates who have already obtained a diploma.

Established in 2007, the School of Education (SOE) at the Johns Hopkins University has quickly taken its place as a national leader in education reform through research and teaching. Grounded in the Johns Hopkins tradition of research and innovation, SOE currently receives more funded research than any other graduate school of education in the United States.

The Talent Development Secondary has been a partner for change in hundreds of schools in cities, suburbs, and rural areas across the country. During the 2014-15 school year, we worked with 54 schools serving nearly 50,000 students, with 23 high schools and 31 middle schools in 14 states and the District of Columbia.

Since 1981, the Morris Heights Health Center (MHHC) has had a record of distinction as the major provider of health care to Morris Heights and the surrounding areas. Born out of the local need for quality care, MHHC is a non-profit organization funded by federal, state, and foundation grants as well as private and corporate donors.

The New York City Department of Education (NYCDOE) is the department of the government of New York City that manages the city's public school system. The City School District of the City of New York (the New York City public schools) is the largest school system in the United States, with over 1.1 million students taught in more than 1,800 separate schools. The department covers all five boroughs of New York City, and has an annual budget of nearly 25 billion dollars. For more information, visit NYCDOE.
